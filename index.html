CLIENT — SWISSGEAR × GROUP III INTERNATIONAL

Whitespace Opportunity & Brand Strategy

LIVE ENGAGEMENT · NORTHWESTERN IQ CONSULTING

The problem — SWISSGEAR needed to find an underserved whitespace in the mobility/travel gear category, where most competitor innovation is built narrowly around air travel.

The solution — Built a whitespace framework mapping mobility along two axes — scarcity-driven vs. optionality-driven need states, single-occasion vs. cross-occasion adaptability — layered with TAM/SAM/SOM sizing and a category PESTLE scan.

The impact — Gave the client a sharper, non-obvious lens for evaluating whitespace beyond a standard competitor grid — reframing "gear gaps" as identity and readiness gaps.

My contribution — Led the framework development end-to-end: built the 2×2 from a psychology-first hypothesis, ran the TAM/SAM/SOM sizing, and managed data requests with the client contact under NDA.

GROUP PROJECT — BRAND COMMUNICATION DECISIONS

Dyson — Brand Audit & Extension Strategy

The problem — Dyson's brand equity is built almost entirely on vacuums and engineering credibility. The question: is that equity coherent enough to extend, and into what?

The solution — Ran a full brand audit — stated identity vs. real consumer perception (reviews, Reddit, TikTok) — then tested extension categories via survey and depth interviews. Landed on kitchen appliances: absent from the portfolio, consistent with the engineering identity, backed by category growth.

The impact — A dual-horizon recommendation: kitchen appliances short-term, a broader home-ecosystem vision long-term — grounded in stated identity and consumer-validated data, not a guess.

My contribution — Owned the audit framework and its Goal/Purpose framing; designed the primary research instruments (survey + depth-interview guide probing brand-equity transfer and true purchase intent); tied the workstreams together into the final recommendation, evaluation strategy, and synthesis.

GROUP PROJECT — BRAND COMMUNICATION DECISIONS

Pinterest — Collage Ads Digital Brand Strategy

The problem — Alc-bev brands don't naturally think of Pinterest as a media channel. The challenge: build the digital strategy case for Collage Ads, using Angry Orchard as the lead example.

The solution — Built the case around a "planning moment" insight — Pinners plan real occasions before they happen, exactly when a brand wants presence. Used Keller's CBBE pyramid to show equity built in both directions at once.

The impact — One unifying equity statement — occasion ownership — that elevated the pitch from a media buy into a differentiated digital brand strategy argument.

My contribution — Wrote the core positioning arguments (B2B and B2C) and the CBBE translation tying platform behavior to brand equity. On a related version of this project (Tip Top Proper Cocktails), served as closer — pulling platform behavior, category context, and brand justification into one final argument.

GROUP PROJECT — SHOPPER MARKETING

Coors Banquet — Shopper Marketing Activation

The problem — Near-zero trial among men 25–34 who buy beer on habit, not deliberate choice. Needed retailer-specific activations for Jewel-Osco and 7-Eleven.

The solution — Built a shopper insight rooted in identity tension — self-assured men in an uncertain world, resolved by Banquet's constancy — using a Holt cultural-branding lens, translated into differentiated retailer strategies.

The impact — A full-funnel shopper measurement plan, pre-shop email through in-store display through digital engagement, with retailer-specific KPIs proving a "triple win" for shopper, manufacturer, and retailer.

My contribution — Led the upfront insight and strategy work; wrote the core "Because" statement tying the cultural insight to a shopper reason-to-switch; developed the Jewel-Osco retailer strategy end-to-end — connecting insight to tactic to KPI.

CLIENT — GOOGLE

Mapping AI at the Moment of Its Birth

The problem — In 2023, Google had no ground-level read on how Indian consumers were actually using or feeling about Bard — use cases weren't yet defined.

The solution — Ran a longitudinal digital diary study on Recollective, capturing real-time AI interactions over weeks instead of relying on recall.

The impact — An early, ground-level read on AI adoption in one of Google's largest markets, before the company had its own internal framework for it.

My contribution — Co-moderated the digital diary activities and helped synthesize daily entries into adoption patterns and trust barriers.

CLIENT — MONDELEZ (CADBURY SILK)

"Modern Love" — Transmedia Anxiety

The problem — Mondelez needed to understand what "love" means today to inform Silk's Valentine's campaign strategy.

The solution — Ran 9 extended mini-groups and 6 couple depth interviews across 5 relationship-stage cohorts, using a projective technique to surface anxieties a standard focus group wouldn't reach.

The impact — Uncovered "transmedia anxiety" as a defining feature of modern intimacy — directly shifting the Valentine's strategy from grand gestures to small, relatable moments.

My contribution — Designed the recruitment questionnaire and screening criteria per cohort, ran backchecks on high-stakes units, and proposed the projective exercise that became the study's key unlock.

CLIENT — MONDELEZ

Atlas — Mapping the Indian Chocolate Palate

The problem — Mondelez needed to define its product launch strategy by mapping how Indian consumers experience chocolate — a category with no unified sensory language.

The solution — Ran extended product-testing groups across 5 eat-states, benchmarking imported chocolates against existing Silk variants and applying a Kano model to rank preferences.

The impact — Findings became a full NPD brief — pip size, roast quality, melt harmony — translating raw consumer vocabulary into product specification.

My contribution — Created the visual need-state mood boards used in sessions and helped build the data collation structure across this large multi-city study.

CLIENT — VIVO

Project Thames — IoT Market Entry

The problem — Vivo was re-entering the smartwatch/TWS/tablet category after a prior pricing misjudgment and needed a ground-up read on wearable buyers by price segment.

The solution — Ran 49 individual immersions and 10 extended groups across 8 cities, building personas mapped to psychographics, purchase journeys, and feature hierarchies.

The impact — Personas and journey maps directly informed Vivo's category entry sequencing, pricing, and positioning within its IoT roadmap.

My contribution — Managed end-to-end recruitment for the full study, attended immersions across all three categories, and led post-fieldwork persona synthesis for smartwatches specifically.

CLIENT — PIRAMAL

The Autonomy / Wellness Paradox

The problem — Piramal wanted to understand the modern Indian woman's autonomy and health needs across generations, and test a new wellness brand concept.

The solution — Ran qualitative depths and concept testing — surfacing a central tension: as women gained autonomy, their reproductive health quietly declined.

The impact — One tested name ("i choose") was rejected by the target segment as arrogant rather than confident — flagged before further investment, reshaping the brand territory to autonomy and wellness.

My contribution — Flagged the naming risk directly from the data and helped reframe the positioning recommendation that followed.
